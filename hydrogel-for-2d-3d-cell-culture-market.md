<p><p>Hydrogels for 2D & 3D cell culture can be categorized into natural and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels are derived from natural sources such as collagen, gelatin, or alginate, offering biocompatibility and resembling the extracellular matrix. Synthetic hydrogels, on the other hand, are engineered to have specific properties like tunable stiffness or degradation rates. Both types of hydrogels play a crucial role in providing a suitable environment for cell growth and interaction in biomedical research and tissue engineering applications.</p></p>
