<p><p>Bone grafting screws are used in orthopedic surgeries to provide stability and support during bone grafting procedures. There are three main types of bone grafting screws: stainless-steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable. Stainless-steel screws are durable and corrosion-resistant, while titanium screws are lightweight and biocompatible. Bioabsorbable screws are made from materials that gradually dissolve in the body, eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove them. Each type of bone grafting screw offers unique advantages and is chosen based on the specific needs of the patient.</p></p>
