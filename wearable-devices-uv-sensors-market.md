<p><p>Wearable devices with UV sensors are capable of measuring ultraviolet (UV) radiation levels. These sensors are broadly categorized into UVA, UVB, and UVC markets based on the type of UV radiation they detect. UVA sensors primarily measure long-wave UVA rays that penetrate the skin, causing premature aging and increasing skin cancer risk. UVB sensors detect medium-wave UVB rays responsible for sunburns and enhanced skin cancer risk. UVC sensors, on the other hand, are designed to detect short-wave UVC rays, which are naturally filtered out by the Earth's atmosphere and are typically found in artificial sources like germicidal lamps.</p></p>
