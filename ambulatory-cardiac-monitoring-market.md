<p><p>Ambulatory cardiac monitoring refers to the portable devices used to monitor the electrical activity of the heart continuously for an extended period. These systems allow healthcare providers to monitor patients remotely, providing real-time data and alerts for any abnormalities detected. <p><p>The ambulatory cardiac monitoring market consists of different types of devices used for monitoring heart activity outside of traditional healthcare settings. Holter monitors are small devices worn by patients that continuously record their heart's electrical activity over a 24-48 hour period. Event monitors are similar but are only activated by the patient when they experience symptoms. Mobile cardiac telemetry devices are worn for longer periods and provide real-time transmission of heart data to healthcare professionals. Other types of ambulatory cardiac monitors may include patch monitors, implantable loop recorders, and smartphone-based apps.</p></p>
