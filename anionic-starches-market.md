<p><p>Anionic starches are modified starches that have a negative charge, making them useful in various industrial applications. Phosphorylated starch is a type of anionic starch that has been chemically modified with phosphate groups, enhancing its ability to bind water and improve texture in food products. Carboxyalkylated starch, on the other hand, is another type of anionic starch that has been modified with carboxyalkyl groups, providing improved stability and viscosity in applications such as adhesives and papermaking. These two types of anionic starches offer unique properties and benefits in different industries.</p></p>
