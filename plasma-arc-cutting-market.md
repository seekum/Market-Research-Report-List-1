<p><p>Plasma arc cutting is a thermal cutting process that uses an electrically conductive gas to transfer energy from an electrical power source through a plasma cutting torch to the material being cut. This process results in a high-velocity jet of ionized gas that melts the material and expels it away from the cut. Plasma arc cutting is commonly used in industries such as automotive, construction, and manufacturing due to its ability to cut a wide range of materials, including steel, aluminum, and copper, with precision and efficiency.</p><p>The Plasma Arc Cutting Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.6% during the forecast period. <p><p>Plasma arc cutting can be classified into two main market types based on the current used: weak current and strong current. Weak current plasma arc cutting is typically used for cutting thin materials with precision and minimal heat input, making it suitable for delicate applications. On the other hand, strong current plasma arc cutting is used for thicker materials, delivering higher cutting speeds and greater power for heavy-duty industrial applications. Both market types offer unique benefits and cater to different cutting requirements.</p></p>
