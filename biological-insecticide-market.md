<p><p>Biological insecticides are categorized into three main types: microbial pesticide, plant pesticide, and biochemical pesticide. Microbial pesticides are based on microorganisms like bacteria, fungi, and viruses that target specific insects. Plant pesticides are derived from plants and repel or kill insects through natural compounds. Biochemical pesticides are made from natural substances like pheromones or insect growth regulators that disrupt the growth or behavior of insects. Each type offers a natural and eco-friendly alternative to chemical pesticides in pest control.</p></p>
