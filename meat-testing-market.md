<p><p>Meat testing market types include traditional testing and rapid testing. Traditional testing refers to the conventional methods used to analyze meat samples, such as microbial culturing and chemical tests, which are time-consuming and require trained personnel. On the other hand, rapid testing involves the use of advanced technologies like PCR and ELISA to quickly detect contaminants or pathogens in meat samples. Rapid testing methods offer faster results and are suitable for large-scale testing, ensuring food safety and quality. Both types of testing play a crucial role in ensuring the integrity of meat products.</p></p>
