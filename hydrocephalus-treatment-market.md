<p><p>Hydrocephalus is a medical condition characterized by the accumulation of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) in the brain, leading to increased pressure within the skull. The treatment of hydrocephalus typically involves the surgical insertion of a shunt system to divert and manage the flow of CSF. The shunt system consists of a catheter placed in the brain ventricles, a valve to regulate CSF flow, and another catheter that is placed in another area of the body, usually the abdomen, to drain the excess fluid.</p><p>The global hydrocephalus treatment market is projected to experience substantial growth in the coming years. <p><ul><li>Cerebrospinal Fluid Shunt</li><li>Neuroendoscopic Fistula Surgery</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hydrocephalus treatment market types mainly include cerebrospinal fluid shunt and neuroendoscopic fistula surgery. Cerebrospinal fluid shunt refers to a surgical procedure where a device is implanted to drain excess fluid from the brain to another body area, typically the abdomen, to alleviate symptoms of hydrocephalus. On the other hand, neuroendoscopic fistula surgery involves the use of a neuroendoscope to create an alternative pathway for cerebrospinal fluid to bypass the obstruction, reducing fluid buildup and managing hydrocephalus. These treatment approaches aim to reduce the pressure on the brain and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
